Volunteer Stories

Anna’s Story: The Power of Education

"I have always been a firm believer in the transformative power of knowledge as an educator. Volunteering at Haven of Hope provided me with the opportunity to utilize my expertise to instruct survivors in vital life skills, ranging from basic literacy to financial management. Witnessing the newfound confidence in the eyes of survivors who had just acquired the ability to budget or read a book for the first time was an exceptionally enriching experience. I came to the realization that my modest contribution was aiding them in laying the groundwork for a more promising and independent future."


James’ Journey: Providing Legal Aid

"As a student of law, I aimed to apply my expertise in a meaningful way. At Haven of Hope, I offered my services to support survivors with legal matters, such as initiating protection orders and managing divorce proceedings. Although it presented its challenges, the knowledge that I was aiding individuals in securing their freedom and safety was incredibly gratifying. I witnessed firsthand the vital role that legal assistance plays in the journey to healing, and this is an experience that will remain with me indefinitely."


Lerato’s Story: Healing Through Art

"Art has always been my passion, and I’ve long believed in its power to heal. At Haven of Hope, I started an art therapy program for survivors, where they could express their emotions through painting, drawing, and crafts. The transformation I witnessed in the participants was nothing short of amazing. Some who were initially hesitant to share their stories found their voice through art. It’s been an honor to provide a creative outlet that helps them heal and regain their sense of self."


David’s Commitment: Ensuring Safety

"As a retired security professional, I wanted to give back in a way that utilized my experience. Volunteering at Haven of Hope, I helped enhance the security protocols at their safe houses. Knowing that I was helping to create a safe and secure environment for survivors to rebuild their lives gave me a sense of purpose. It’s more than just providing physical security; it’s about giving peace of mind to those who have been through so much."


Thandi’s Passion: Cooking with Love

"I have always loved cooking and wanted to share that love in a meaningful way. At Haven of Hope, I volunteered to cook meals for the survivors. It was not just about providing nourishment; it was about offering comfort and care through food. I saw how a warm meal could brighten someone’s day, and it was incredible to see how something so simple could make such a big impact. The kitchen became a place of warmth, community, and healing."


"Nokuthula’s Dedication: Building Confidence

"With a background in personal development, I volunteered to lead empowerment workshops at Haven of Hope. The goal was to help survivors build self-esteem and develop leadership skills. Witnessing the transformation in participants was deeply inspiring. Women who once doubted themselves began to stand tall, speak confidently, and take control of their lives. It reminded me of the resilience within us all and the power of support and encouragement.

Michael’s Involvement: Mentoring Young Survivors

"As a mentor, I’ve always been passionate about guiding young people. At Haven of Hope, I volunteered to mentor young survivors, many of whom had experienced trauma at a very young age. We talked about everything from school to their dreams for the future. Over time, I saw these young people regain hope and start to believe in themselves again. Being a positive male role model in their lives was both a responsibility and a privilege I’ll never forget."


Zinhle’s Contribution: Enhancing Digital Skills

"With a background in IT, I wanted to help survivors gain valuable skills for the modern world. I volunteered to teach basic computer skills and digital literacy at Haven of Hope. For many, this was their first time using a computer, and seeing their excitement as they learned how to send emails, create documents, and explore new opportunities online was incredibly rewarding. These skills are more than just technical; they open doors to independence and new beginnings."


Peter’s Efforts: Creating a Healing Garden

"Gardening has always been therapeutic for me. I decided to volunteer by creating a healing garden at one of Haven of Hope’s safe houses. Together with some of the survivors, we planted flowers, vegetables, and herbs. The garden became a place of reflection and tranquility where survivors could connect with nature and find peace. It was amazing to see how nurturing the garden also nurtured their spirits, providing a sense of accomplishment and hope."


Sibongile’s Initiative: Organizing Community Outreach

"I've always been passionate about community engagement, so when I volunteered at Haven of Hope, I focused on organizing outreach programs. We worked with local schools and communities to raise awareness about GBV and the resources available. Seeing the community come together, learn, and take action to prevent GBV was incredibly empowering. It reminded me that change starts with education and awareness, and I was proud to be a part of that change."
